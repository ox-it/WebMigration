PC Applications Development

In addition to rolling out core business systems purchased from external vendors, IT Services provides smaller bespoke applications for UAS, typically for data capture/manipulation, which are developed in-house through the PC Applications team in Software Solutions.

What we do

The PC Applications team uses a rapid systems development methodology which means that once started we can develop a working system for you in typically 1-3 months calendar time. It adds value by plugging gaps in functionality - either by serving a precise need for which no package solution exists, or by providing additional Oxford-specific functionality to complement one of the larger business systems.

Thus applications may be standalone - handling their own unique datasets, or they may be developed to work alongside a core business system (e.g. OSS or OPENdoor) - taking data outputs from these systems and providing additional ways to manipulate the data externally.

In practice, most applications are built in Microsoft Access which provides the management/administrative functionality and often have a web front end to capture data or for users to use the system. IT Services is looking into other technologies to either replace or complement Microsoft Access to develop these types of systems.

We also continually review the systems we have previously developed to see if any of them can be replaced as new and improved core business systems are implemented.

Systems used widely by departments and colleges

PC Applications is also responsible for several systems used widely by departments and colleges.

  • Resolve - Used by departments to support the submission of research grant applications. Ongoing until replaced by ‘X5’ project.
  • ADMIT - A system for undergraduate colleges to assist their admissions process. Ongoing until the successor to OSS comes on stream.
  • Mark-It - Used by several faculties to support the undergraduate examination marking process. Ongoing until the successor to OSS comes on stream.

  • The ability for UAS staff to generate their own personalised letterheads.
  • A library of common UAS words and phrases under the autotext facility.
  • The ability to generate sets of distribution labels which match the email distribution lists in our email system.

For general help on Office products and the additional Word functionality please visit the UAS Office Support website. If you are still having problems then email BSPHelpdesk@admin.ox.ac.uk.

How we work with you

Once it has been decided that the system should be developed by PC Applications the development is an iterative process:

  1. First we meet with you to gather your requirements. Based on this we prepare a Statement of Work (SOW), which is a short document describing our understanding of your high-level requirements, how we will provide the solution and other information relevant to the development. We expect the person who requested the system to sign off this document before we commence development.
  2. We then build the first version and show you what we have done.
  3. This usually leads to some refinements and sometimes gathering additional requirements.
  4. We then build a further version and again show you what we have done.
  5. This continues, subject to time and resource constraints, until we have developed the system as you want it.
  6. We then expect you to thoroughly test the system.
  7. Once you are happy with the system we ask you to sign it off as meeting your agreed requirements.
  8. We then continue to support your system and fix any bugs that may be found. We also undertake any changes you require, although this work is dependent on priority and our current workload.

Testing

Testing comprises:

  • Feedback from the customer during the iterative design-and-build process.
  • Internal checks against QA standards undertaken by the development team at each iteration.
  • Testing of the final product by the development team to verify that it meets the requirements you identified
  • Testing of the final product by the customer – for which we will help you design a testing process.

Post rollout

Following delivery of the application, IT Serviceswill continue to support and maintain it and fix any bugs arising. This is undertaken by the same team as developed the application, to ensure familiar and continuity.
