Project methodology

The BSP project methodology is scalable to suit projects of all sizes and types. It is equally applicable to projects based on commercial software and to the in-house development of bespoke software.

The methodology comprises:

  • Three phases; ten steps
  • A pictorial representation of the 10 steps
  • Standard projects versus micro projects

Standard projects versus micro projects

BSP distinguishes two types of project based on scale:

  • Standard projects – which may be either in-house development or commercial off-the-shelf package implementations. Standard projects are substantial projects requiring full documentation and deliverables at each of the ten steps.
  • Micro projects – these are smaller, simpler, lower cost and quicker. Project management still passes through each of the ten steps, but bypasses many of the interim deliverables. This enables the project to move swiftly into the design stage.
