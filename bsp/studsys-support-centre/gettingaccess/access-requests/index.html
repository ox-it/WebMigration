Arranging access to OSS - Guidance for staff users

Staff users need to follow these 5 steps, in order:

  • Step 1. Make contact with your local Information Custodian
  • Step 2. Fill in an access request form
  • Step 3. Get your form authorised and submitted
  • Step 4. Arrange training
  • Step 5. Ask your local IT Officer to configure your PC

Step 1. Make contact with your local Information Custodian

Each area of the collegiate University has an Information Custodianwho ensures that Student Administration data is appropriately managed and protected. It is the role of the Information Custodian to ensure that individual users have the right authorisation to access those areas of OSS relevant to their role; that they have attended the relevant training; and that they are kept up to date with changes that may impact working practices. Refer to the List of Information Custodians (140kb) to identify who this person is for your area. Make contact with them to establish what level of access to OSS you need to do your job.

Step 3. Get your form authorised and submitted

There are 3 ways you can do this:

1) Ask your Information Custodian to submit an electronic copy of your form, by e-mail, to: oss@admin.ox.ac.uk. Your Information Custodian's email address will act as authorisation. Alternatively:

2) Ask your Information Custodian to sign a hard copy of your form and fax it to the OSS Support Centre (fax: 01865 (2) 84880). Their signature will act as authorisation. Alternatively:

3) Ask your Information Custodian to sign a hard copy of your form, then take this with you when you attend your training.

Once set-up has been completed, one of the following things will happen:

  • If you are a new user: OSS Support will email you with instructions on how to access OSS using your Oxford Single Sign-on username and password. Responsibilites requested with 'update' access will be added, once training has been completed.
  • If you are an existing user requesting additional access: The responsibility will be added once training has been completed for update access, or upon completion of the request for read-only access.
  • If you are an Information Custodian requesting multi-user access: Information Custodian will receive an email from OSS Support confirming the users that have been set up with access. An email will also be sent direct to each user providing them with:

    • Instructions on how to log in for the first time using their Oxford Single Sign-on username and password;
    • Information on using the OSS Data Views and links to useful documentation;
    • Training available;
    • OSS Support contact details.

Please note: Unused OSS accounts will be end-dated after 400 days of inactivity.

Step 4. Arrange training

Book yourself onto the appropriate training. Details are on the Training section of this site. If you require read-only access to data then training is not mandatory - although it is advised.

Back to top

Step 5. Ask your local IT Officer to configure your PC

Ask your local IT Officer to configure your PC to connect you to the Oracle Student System. In order that they can do this, you will need to direct them to the Guidance for IT Officers page on this website.
