Generic roles in BSP


  • Programme Manager
  • Project Manager
  • Business Analyst
  • Functional Developer
  • Database Administrator (DBA)

Programme Manager

Responsible for:

  • Planning and designing the programme and actively monitoring its overall progress, resolving issues and initiating corrective action as appropriate.
  • Supporting the Programme Sponsor in achieving the relevant approval to proceed with the programme.
  • Maintaining overall integrity and coherence of the programme, and developing and maintaining the programme environment to support each individual project within it.
  • Ensuring that the programme achieves the objectives that have been set and that agreed benefits have been realised.
  • Managing the programme's budget, monitoring the expenditure and costs against benefits that are realised as the programme progresses.
  • Working alongside the business to recognise opportunities across a very broad front and recommending ways of exploiting these so that senior managers feel they have been able to make informed decisions.

Project Manager

Responsible for:

  • Understanding the business issues and data challenges of the University context.
  • Creating and executing project plans, revising as appropriate to meet changing needs and requirements.
  • Managing operational aspects of the project; managing priorities and scope on a day-to-day basis.
  • Applying BSP project methodology and QA procedures and enforcing project standards.
  • Minimising project exposure to risks and issues.
  • Developing and sustaining positive working relationships between BSP and the user community.
  • Developing and documenting detailed proposals for long-term BSP projects, responding to policy and process decisions made by the collegiate University.

Business Analyst

Responsible for:

  • Assisting the customer and project team in defining project scope, objectives and Key Performance Indicators.
  • Identifying, documenting and analysing the necessary information and data to inform practicable solutions to improving business processes.
  • Working consultatively with end users and the business to identify needs, priorities and potential improvements over current workflows/processes.
  • Gathering, evaluating and confirming detailed requirements through interviews, research, scenario-building, surveys, site visits and workshops.
  • Recommending different solutions that meet these requirements, explaining pros and cons and where value is added.
  • Preparing functional specifications and system design specifications.
  • Developing accurate and complete system test plans.
  • Assisting the customer in managing the change within their department.

Functional Developer

Responsible for:

  • Examining user requirements and formulating a response to meet these which is technically sound.
  • Implementing enhancements of existing functionality and/or provisions of new functionality in accordance with development plans.
  • Identifying and logging bugs or errors when encountered, and determining a course of action towards resolution.
  • Identifying where upgrades and patches are required, and - when system upgrades or patches are applied - undertaking appropriate testing.
  • Working with Helpdesk and Training Team to provide pro-active user support.

Database Administrator (DBA)

Responsible for:

  • Working within in team to develop a robust, secure and resilient technical infrastructure for the University’s core business systems.
  • Working with operations staff, business analysts and key users to provide a consistent and responsive support service for dealing with faults and incidents.
  • Problem-solving and responding, where required, to users’ requests for assistance.
  • Ensuring, in conjunction with operations staff, that the core business systems and associated hardware and software are optimised and administered to deliver a consistent performance.
  • Keeping abreast of emerging technologies for the development and support for robust business systems and peripheral devices.
  • Responding as a member of a team to system downtime and times of crisis.

Helpdesk Operator

Responsible for:

  • Providing the first point of contact for users seeking assistance, usually by telephone or email.
  • Maintaining the call-logging procedures, recording details of all calls received, and utilising responses to build up an effective knowledge base for use within the team.
  • Agreeing priority levels and response times with users in accordance with the obligations of the Service Level Agreement (SLA). Following up, and ensuring that queries are answered within the appropriate SLA time.
  • Prioritising multiple incidents and queries, whilst also seeking to prioritise tasks to achieve customer satisfaction.
  • Monitoring reported incidents and identifying trends and common problems; working with the appropriate departments to eliminate these or develop a standard solution. 
  • Providing some user training either one-to-one or in small groups, and assisting in the production of training and support documentation.
  • Alerting users via the web, email and other appropriate means to system problems, major developments and other changes.

Trainer

Responsible for:

  • Working closely with the project and business teams to ensure that training is fully involved throughout the project lifecycle, from initiation to project closure.
  • Scoping the training requirements for project deliverables by contributing to the training strategy, identifying training needs and target audiences and creating tailored training programmes for each audience.
  • Developing interactive training courses and exercises, as well as creating paper and online learning materials for each project, which include IT steps and business processes.
  • Delivering training courses for each project, which may include face-to-face training to groups, one-to-one coaching, floor-walking, roadshows and online events.
  • Monitoring and evaluating delegate feedback during the implementation, in order to improve future courses.
  • Handing over training courses and learning materials to the sponsoring business teams, so they are able to provide future training courses.
